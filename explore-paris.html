Explore Paris - Everything you need to know

This guide covers everything you need to know before traveling to The City of Love. From flight fare, to hotels, resturants, recreational centers and many more.

Nemerem Favour
Oct 10th, 2023 • 6063 views

I have traveled Paris (and nearby cities) on three separate occasions, learning more about what makes this a genuinely magnificent city with amazing people and a great culture.

Mobility within the City (and beyond)

Traveling in Paris can be stressful and expensive (for parking), so avoid driving if possible. If you must rent a car (if you are driving to other parts of France or Europe), try renting from one of the train stations on the outer edges of the city (and just take the Metro to the train station). This will save on the stress of navigating Paris streets. - There is an extensive network of trains, buses, and even water taxis on the river, so it is very easy to get around.

Airports
There are two major airports in Paris. Charles De Gaulle (to the northeast) and Orly airport (to the south). Both are well connected to the city and have flights to many destinations. CDG is a larger airport, so more flights will probably land there, especially from other continents.

Train Stations
If you want to take a day trip from Paris, or are arriving by train from another location, make sure you check which train station you are arriving at. There are six major train stations in Paris, each managing trains that depart towards different regions of France and other countries.
Gard du Nord is, in the 10th Arr., where the Eurostar from London arrives/departs, as well as other high-speed trains to Brussels, Amsterdam, Cologne, etc.

Museums

You can schedule your visit time/purchase tickets for most museums. You are guaranteed a time and avoid the long lines to buy tickets/wait for space to open, so I highly recommend doing this, especially for the big museums like the Louvre and Orsay. Some smaller ones might not be as busy, especially if you are traveling in the off-season.

1. Louvre Museum

Allow 3-4 hours to spend in the museum, as the building is massive and there is a lot of art to see. Try to visit as early as possible, and if you are in the opening time slot, go straight to the Mona Lisa if you want pictures without waiting in a long line.

2. Musée National Picasso-Paris

From the web: 17th-century building holding 5,000 artworks by Pablo Picasso, his own art collection & archive.Try to visit as early as possible, and if you are in the opening time slot, go straight to the Mona Lisa if you want pictures without waiting in a long line.
